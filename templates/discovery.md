## 1 · Root Problem + Evidence

**Bar:** a root problem distinct from the stated request, backed by evidence the
client can produce. Anecdote alone fails.

**Ask:**
- What made this a priority *now* rather than six months ago?
- What happens to the business if we do nothing for twelve months?
- How are you measuring the pain today? Show me the number.
- Who inside the company disagrees that this is the problem?

## 2 · Why This Platform

**Bar:** the platform choice is either justified on business grounds we can
defend, or formally challenged with our alternative and their response recorded.
Silent agreement fails.

**Ask:**
- Who chose this platform, and what was the reasoning?
- What did you rule out, and why?
- Who maintains this after launch? What is their skill level?
- Is this decision reversible, and at what cost?

| If we disagree — the trade-off we presented | |

## 3 · Budget Range + Decision Authority

**Bar:** a workable range **and** a named signer. Either missing fails — route
back to Business Development, do not proceed to proposal.

**Ask:**
- What range has been set aside for this? (Offer a bracket if they stall.)
- Who signs the contract? Is that person in this conversation?
- Has anything been budgeted for maintenance after launch?
- What else is competing for this money this quarter?

| Signer name + title | |

## 8 · Red Flags

Conditions the gates above do not catch. Two or more forces a documented
justification before Go.

- [ ] Timeline fixed to an external date with no scope flexibility
- [ ] "Just build what the competitor has"
- [ ] Previous vendor terminated mid-project, reason unclear
- [ ] Scope described as "simple" while listing 4+ integrations
- [ ] Payment terms contested before a proposal exists
- [ ] Decision-making spread across a committee with no chair
- [ ] Requests spec work or free design before contract
- [ ] Values sit against ours in a way we cannot reconcile
